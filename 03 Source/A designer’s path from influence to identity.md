# A designer’s path from influence to identity

## A designer’s path from influence to identity / 设计师从受影响到建立身份的成长之路

![[10 Attachment/20b4fc29c39004d3d17367340ea8b1bc_MD5.webp]]

As designers, we are told to search for our style, as if it were a fixed destination waiting at the end of a road. But style is less a discovery and more a discipline. It does not arrive in a sudden moment of recognition; it accumulates slowly, in repetitions and refinements, in the daily work of noticing and choosing.

作为设计师，我们被告知要寻找自己的风格，仿佛它是道路尽头等待着的一个固定终点。但风格与其说是一种发现，不如说是一种自律。它不会在某个突然的觉醒时刻降临；它是通过不断的重复与完善，在日常的观察与选择中缓慢积累而成的。

---

What begins as imitation hardens over time into something recognisable, a voice, an eye, and what we call the style of a designer.

起初的模仿随着时间的推移而凝固成某种可辨识的东西——一种声音、一种眼光，以及我们称之为设计师风格的东西。

---

## Repetition builds instinct, but instinct builds signature / 重复建立本能，而本能铸就个人特色

![[10 Attachment/f6b5acc27935f185935c986fc04faf01_MD5.webp]]

Do something often enough, and it becomes second nature. But the nuance here is that if you’re just mindlessly repeating it, you’ll plateau.

当你把一件事做得足够多，它就会成为你的第二天性。但其中的微妙之处在于，如果你只是盲目地重复，你就会陷入瓶颈期。

---

Style doesn’t work when it isn’t metabolised

未经“代谢”的风格是行不通的

---

Everyone borrows when they start. But if the reference never gets broken down and absorbed, it shows. The problem is when the reference never passes through your own reasoning. Referencing designs without asking why they work, why they look the way they do, why they make sense in this context it just hardwires muscle memory, not mastery.

每个人初学时都会借鉴。但如果参考资料从未被分解和吸收，它就会显露出来。**问题在于参考资料从未经过你自己的思考。参考设计而不去问为什么要这样设计、为什么它们看起来是那样、为什么它们在特定的语境下有意义，这只会固化肌肉记忆，而不是掌握真谛。**

---

That’s where an aesthetic stops being imitation and starts carrying weight.

只有当审美不再仅仅是模仿时，它才开始产生力量。

---

## How you can shape your aesthetic / 如何塑造你的审美

![[10 Attachment/661a5be68008e03b40dd7dc47348cf95_MD5.webp]]

## 1\. Curate a reference library / 1. 策划一个参考库

Start treating inspiration like data, collect every design, photo, space, or product that you find interesting, it’s a clue about what your taste responds to. This collection is your visual vocabulary.

开始把灵感当作数据来对待，收集你认为有趣的每一个设计、照片、空间或产品，这是关于你品味偏好的线索。这个集合就是你的视觉词汇表。

---

Over time, you’ll start to see patterns in what you like and in why you like it. Those patterns are the raw material for your aesthetic. The more intentional your collection, the clearer your taste becomes.

随着时间的推移，你会开始发现你喜欢什么以及为什么喜欢的模式。这些模式是你审美的原材料。你的收集越是有意为之，你的品味就越清晰。

---

![[10 Attachment/29f24c1577a27314168bf7cabf118368_MD5.webp]]

Collect and tag designs that speak to you to start seeing patterns in your taste. ( Source )

**收集并标记引起你共鸣的设计，开始发现你品味中的模式。**（ 来源 ）

---

## 2\. Take cross-disciplinary inputs / 2. 吸收跨学科的输入

When references are limited to the same Dribbble boards or product screenshots, the outcome is inevitably a recombination of what’s already familiar.

当参考资料仅限于同样的 Dribbble 面板或产品截图时，产出的结果不可避免地只是已有事物的重新组合。

---

To move past that loop, start observing designs outside of your domain of work. Good design is in everything you do on a daily basis.

要跳出这个循环，请开始观察你工作领域之外的设计。好的设计存在于你日常生活的方方面面。

---

It can be architecture, TV show visuals, your electronic gadgets, photography, and other everyday encounters.

它可以是建筑、电视节目视觉效果、你的电子设备、摄影以及其他日常接触的事物。

---

You must keep expanding your perceptual framework.

你必须不断扩展你的感知框架。

---

![[10 Attachment/ca6eeab7f6eceef8a2495aab26f88810_MD5.webp]]

Look beyond your field: Films, architecture, photography, everyday visuals can all sharpen your eye. This is the intro visual of Only murders in the building TV show.

开阔视野：**电影、建筑、摄影、日常视觉效果都能磨炼你的眼光。**这是电视剧《大楼里的谋杀案》的片头视觉效果。

---

> Hamsika, product designer at Canvs, shared her thoughts on how she builds her aesthetic:
>
> Canvs 的产品设计师 Hamsika 分享了她在构建审美方面的见解：

---

> “I build my aesthetic from the stuff I naturally enjoy. A lot of it comes from the movies and shows I watch, especially thrillers and mysteries, which push me toward darker, minimal, cinematic styles. I also pull ideas from my surroundings, just noticing little patterns, textures, or details in everyday life.
>
> “我从自己自然喜欢的事物中构建我的审美。很多灵感来源于我看的电影和节目，尤其是惊悚片和悬疑片，它们促使我倾向于更阴郁、极简且具有电影感的风格。我也从周围环境中汲取灵感，留意日常生活中微小的模式、纹理或细节。

---

> Sometimes I’ll click a photo, take a screenshot, or save it on a Pinterest board so I can come back to it later. And over time, these things start to shape the type of aesthetic I prefer.”
>
> 有时我会拍张照片、截个图，或者保存到 Pinterest 上，以便以后查阅。久而久之，这些点滴开始塑造我偏好的审美类型。”

---

## 3\. Reverse-engineer work you admire / 3. 对你赞赏的作品进行逆向工程

Take a design that you love and dissect it.

选择一个你喜欢的设计并剖析它。

---

Ask yourself *why* it works, again and again, until you get to the root of what makes it compelling.

不断自问它*为什么*有效，直到找到让它具有吸引力的根本原因。

---

Once you understand the mechanics behind what draws you in, you can start applying those insights to your own projects, without ever directly imitating. Extract the reasoning and let it inform your own aesthetic.

一旦你理解了吸引你的背后的机制，你就可以开始将这些见解应用到你自己的项目中，而无需直接模仿。提取其中的逻辑，让它为你的个人审美提供灵感。

---

![[10 Attachment/4d8911aeac1cbab43548a72a79ed0e76_MD5.webp]]

Dissect products and designs you love to understand the reasoning behind what works. ( Source )

剖析你喜欢的产品和设计，了解其成功背后的逻辑。（ 来源 ）

## 4\. Make fast, low-stakes experiments / 4. 进行快速、低风险的实验

Don’t wait for a big project to try something new. Set up small, low-pressure experiments, mockups, personal projects, or even tweaks to existing work. Each experiment will teach something concrete about your taste and how certain choices work in context. Over time, these tiny trials will accumulate into a much clearer sense of what feels like *yours*.

不要等到大项目才尝试新事物。开展一些小型、无压力的实验、原型、个人项目，甚至是对比现有作品的微调。每一次实验都会让你对自己的品味以及特定选择在特定语境下的效果有具体认识。随着时间的推移，这些微小的尝试会积累成一种更清晰的属于“你”的感觉。

---

![[10 Attachment/1e1bc708a0235b73c59dae73d7d6030b_MD5.webp]]

Instead of learning to build a whole product from scratch, start with something small, like card and icon design. ( Source )

与其学习从头开始构建整个产品，不如从小处着手，比如卡片和图标设计。（ 来源 ）

---

> Akhil, who works as a product designer at Canvs, shared his thoughts on how he develops his aesthetic -
>
> 在 Canvs 担任产品设计师的 Akhil 分享了他发展个人审美的见解：

---

> “For me, developing a personal aesthetic is like training a muscle. It’s part of my daily routine. I make sure to spend at least 5 minutes every day looking at good design and art (even outside my field) to train my eyes, the same way we train our brain. I have a spare Instagram account. So if I come across anything good, I just forward it to that account and check it all over the weekend. I also experiment with new products, replicate designs in Figma or on paper, and then add my own twist. It’s not something you “get” over a weekend, it grows with daily practice and curiosity.”
>
> “对我来说，培养个人审美就像锻炼肌肉。这是我日常生活的一部分。我确保每天至少花 5 分钟观察优秀的设计和艺术（甚至是本领域之外的），以锻炼我的眼光，就像我们锻炼大脑一样。我有一个备用的 Instagram 账号。如果我发现任何好的东西，我就会转发到那个账号，并在周末统一查看。我还会尝试新产品，在 Figma 或纸上复制设计，然后加入我自己的创意。这不是一个周末就能‘搞定’的事情，它随着日常的练习和好奇心而成长。”

---

## Aesthetic as a muscle, not a moment you arrive at / 审美是一块肌肉，而非一个抵达的时刻

You can’t develop an aesthetic in neat steps. It’s messy, inconsistent, and often frustrating. One day a combination clicks, the next it feels off. What matters is that you keep doing your work, questioning it, and letting your taste evolve. You’ll notice patterns in what draws you in, and slowly those instincts start guiding decisions without conscious effort.

你无法通过整齐的步骤来发展审美。它是杂乱、不连贯且常常令人沮丧的。某一天一个组合成功了，第二天又感觉不对。重要的是你坚持工作，不断质疑，并让你的品味演变。你会注意到吸引你的模式，慢慢地，这些直觉会开始在无意识中指导你的决策。
